Food for Thought

Mar 2nd, 2005

<p>Here’s a list I just received of some interesting items. Most might already be widely known, if not all of them, but they still made me smile. Read on.</p>

<!– more –>

<p><em>Why the sun lightens our hair, but darkens our skin?</em></p>

<p><em>Why women can’t put on mascara with their mouth closed?</em></p>

<p><em>Why you don’t ever see the headline: "Psychic Wins Lottery"?</em></p>

<p><em>Why "abbreviated" is such a long word?</em></p>

<p><em>Why Doctors call what they do "practice"?</em></p>

<p><em>Why you have to click on "Start" to stop Windows 98?</em></p>

<p><em> Why lemon juice is made with artificial flavor, while dishwashing liquid is made with real lemons?</em></p>

<p><em>Why the man who invests all your money is called a "Broker"?</em></p>

<p><em>Why there isn’t mouse flavored cat food?</em></p>

<p><em>Who tastes dog food when it has a "new & improved" flavor?</em></p>

<p><em>Why Noah didn’t swat those two mosquitoes?</em></p>

<p><em>Why they sterilize the needle for lethal injections?</em></p>

<p><em>Why they don’t make the whole plane out of the material used for the indestructible black box?</em></p>

<p><em>Why sheep don’t shrink when it rains?</em></p>

<p><em>Why they are called apartments when they are all stuck together?</em></p>

<p><em>If con is the opposite of pro, is Congress the opposite of progress?</em></p>

<p><em>Why they call the airport "the terminal" if flying is so safe?</em></p>

<p><em>AND…</em></p>

<p><em>&nbsp;</em></p>

<p><em>In case you need further proof that the human race is doomed because of stupidity, here are some actual label instructions on consumer goods:</em></p>

<p><em>&nbsp;</em></p>

<p><em><u>On a Myer hairdryer:</u></em></p>

<p><em>-"Do not use while sleeping".</em></p>

<p><em>(Darn, and that’s the only time I have to work on my hair).</em></p>

<p><em><u>On a bag of Chips:</u></em></p>

<p><em>-â€œYou could be a winner!&nbsp; No purchase necessary.&nbsp; Details insideâ€?.</em></p>

<p><em>(The shoplifter special?)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On a bar of Palmolive soap:</u></em></p>

<p><em>-"Directions:&nbsp; Use like regular soap".</em></p>

<p><em>(And that would be how???)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On some frozen dinners:</u></em></p>

<p><em>-"Serving suggestion:&nbsp; Defrost".</em></p>

<p><em>(But, it’s just a suggestion).</em></p>

<p><em><u>On Nanna’s Tiramisu dessert (printed on bottom):</u></em></p>

<p><em>- "Do not turn upside down".</em></p>

<p><em>(Well…duh, a bit late, huh)!</em></p>

<p><em><u>On Marks & Spencer Bread Pudding:</u></em></p>

<p><em>-"Product will be hot after heating".</em></p>

<p><em>(And you thought????…)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On packaging for a K-Mart iron:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Do not iron clothes on body".</em></p>

<p><em>(But wouldn’t this save me more time?)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On Boot’s Children Cough Medicine:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Do not drive a car or operate machinery after taking this medication".</em></p>

<p><em>(We could do a lot to reduce the rate of construction accidents if we could just get those 5 year olds with head colds off those forklifts.)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On Nytol Sleep Aid:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Warning: May cause drowsiness".</em></p>

<p><em>(And…I’m taking this because???)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On most brands of Christmas lights:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"For indoor or outdoor use only".</em></p>

<p><em>(As opposed to…what?)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On a Japanese food processor:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Not to be used for the other use".</em></p>

<p><em>(Now, somebody out there, help me on this.&nbsp; I’m a bit curious.)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On Nobby’s peanuts:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Warning: contains nuts".</em></p>

<p><em>(Talk about a news flash!)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On an American Airlines packet of nuts:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Instructions:&nbsp; Open packet, eat nuts".</em></p>

<p><em>(Step 3: maybe, uh…fly Delta?)</em></p>

<p><em><u>On a Swedish chainsaw:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Do not attempt to stop chain with your hands or genitals".</em></p>

<p><em>(Was there a lot of this happening somewhere?)</em></p>

<p><em> * I don’t blame the company, I blame the parents for this one:</em></p>

<p><em><u>On a child’s superman costume:</u></em></p>

<p><em>"Wearing of this garment does not enable you to fly".</em></p>

<p><em>&nbsp;</em></p>

<p><em>Now that you’ve smiled at least once, it’s your turn to spread the stupidity and send this to someone you want to bring a smile to (maybe even</em></p>

<p><em>a chuckle)…in other words send it to everyone.&nbsp; We all need to smile every once in a while.</em></p>

<p>I hope it made you smile too :)</p>
