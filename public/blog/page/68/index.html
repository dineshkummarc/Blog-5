Managing the Worlds (Financial) Information

Jun 18th, 2005

Great post by John Battelle about unconfirmed rumors that Google will offer something similar to eBay’s PayPal. Check it out: Google to Offer Payment Service

The first reaction would be "hÃ¤? what does that have to do with search?"… but then you notice, they are not about search. Never have been. Their mission "<I>is to organize the world’s information and make it universally accessible and useful</I>". And boy is my buying habits a big part of that. It might be a bit scary though knowing that Google is known to not really share what they do with all the information. Hyped Hype

Jun 18th, 2005

I have to fully agree with Fight the Bull, who posted a press release from SAP in their post entitled: We can’t make this stuff up:

<blockquote cite="http://www.fightthebull.com/blog/2005/04/we-cant-make-this-stuff-up.html"><p>100 industry-specific analytic applications that empower users with innovative new ways to drive core processes and business decisions based on actionable business insight. SAP Analytics are a new breed of model-driven composite applications that change the analytic application playing field across more than 25 industries. </p></blockquote>

I absolutely agree with Jon in that this really finally explains to us what analytical applications are. Companies seriously have to think about writing stuff in a language that other people can understand. Sadly that also means that they can’t build some products and have the PR and Marketing department build some meaning and reason around it.
